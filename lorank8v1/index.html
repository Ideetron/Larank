System Info

The Lorank comes with three forwarders preinstalled. Per default, the poly packet forwarder is running. This forwarder is derived from the Semtech standard forwarder and maintained by Ideetron and supported by The Things Network. With this forwarder it is possible to send your data to one or multiple (none OTAA) servers and get insight in its performance on the status page of this website. The Semtech standard packet forwarder is simpler, also supported by The Things Network, but not maintained by Ideetron. Getting your data from Semtech may require an account, and its operations can only be monitored by checking the logs regularly. Finally, you can use the packet forwarder from Loriot. This is a closed source forwarder and sends the data directly to the Loriot backend. Data and statistics can be obtaind at their website. An account must be made first. Some (or all) of the configuration settings on this page may not work for the Semtech or Loriot forwarder.

To switch, stop the concentrator, choose and start it again.

It is possible to set a username and password to protect against accidental misuse of this website. However, this offers no protection to eavesdropping, since the password is marshalled in clear text over the network to your gateway. Remember that the Lorank 8 should be operated behind a firewall and shielded from the internet or any other networks you do not trust.

Activation or deactivation of the login facility requires a reboot. Please perform this manually on the 'Admin' page after the credentials have been updated. Just changing the username and/or password does not require a reboot, just an 'update credentials' and is effective immediately. Should you forget the credentials, use ssh to login to the Lorank 8 directly and edit the local_conf.json.

Important: This facility only works with Linux Distro's from 2015 and later, see the Admin page for your version"

If you activate remote control as well, make sure you to tunnel this website over SSL, if you also want to activate access control. There is no other secure way to prohibit eavesdropping in this case.

It is possible 'remote control' this gateway over the Internet, even if it is behind a firewall or NAT. To that end activate the Ngrok service. Note this is a third party service, and you need to get a (free) account to make use of its possibilites. You can expose two endpoints. One is for using SSH to log in (make sure you first change your password!), the other is for the internal website. Although these endpoints are hard to guess, they are public, so the latter may not be safe. For paid accounts there are other possibilities, for example IP Whitelisting, which adds an extra layer of security, see the website Ngrok for more information.

The documentation on the Ngrok website can be somewhat overwheling, but the basic steps are very simple. Note we assume the Lorank has Internet connection, and has already been configured to work properly with the LoRa backend of your choice.

  • First create an account on Ngrok Signup
  • In your dashboard, browse to 'Auth', and copy the "Tunnel Authtoken", paste that below.
  • Select both 'Expose SSH' and 'Expose Website' below, just for now, you can change that later.
  • Also, select in what region your gateway is located (any region will work, but the closer by, the faster the service is)
  • Click 'Update Credentials', reboot your Lorank thereafter and give it a few minutes.
  • Then, browse to the 'Status' page in your Ngrok dashboard. Two tunnels should be visible, something like: tcp://0.tcp.eu.ngrok.io:12345 and http://12345678.eu.ngrok.io/. The latter is clickable and brings you straight to your Lorank Website. The former can be used to log in with SSH on port 12345, so with the command ssh -p 12345 root@0.tcp.eu.ngrok.io'

It can always happen that a (mobile) internet connection dies for some reason. The advise of the carrier in that case usually is: "restart your system", which is kind of hard if you cannot reach it anymore. Therefore, you can activate an 'auto reboot' service, which automatically reboots your Lorank if the internet connection is lost for some time. Value in seconds, with a minumum of 600. Note this applies to the internet connection, not the connection to your Lora backend. For that, use the 'Stall Time' on the Admin page.
